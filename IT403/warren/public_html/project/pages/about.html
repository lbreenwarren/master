What is a Modular Synth?

A modular synth is a type of synthesizer that is designed as to use different components possibly from different manufacturers in order to achieve a wider variety of possibilities. There are many different types of modular synth, the focus of this blog will be on the most popular form, Eurorack, which is a standardization of size & interoperability in this particular subset of modular synths

How do modular synths work?

The functionality of modular synths take inspiration from the earliest synths back in the 1970's and function using patch cables. Today the idea of a synthesizer in most peoples head includes a keyboard with a series of knobs and buttons which change the sound of the keyboard. In the case of keyboard synths, all of the connections between the various components that make up the synthesizer are hard wired beneath the face of the synth. In the case of modular synths, the manufacturers give us access to these connections to send signal to other components which would not normally be possible in a hard wired synth, which allows for more creativity and freedom in making music.

Wow, thats a lot of cables, whats going through them?

There are two types of signals which travel through the patch cable in a synthesizer, both in the form of voltage. The first is audio, which is an electrical signal that is oscillating at an audible rate. The second is control voltage, which can take the form of pulses, oscillations, or envelopes. The only difference between these two types of signal is the frequency and its purpose. An audio signal is what is actually sent to the speakers for the user/audience to hear and control voltage is used to modulate other signals, such as a filter (A filter which filters out high or low frequencies of audio) which can be modulated with control voltage to go from off to fully on at a rate determined by the control voltage. A good way to think of control voltage is to think of it as invisible fingers, in the same way one would slowly turn a knob to have some effect on the sound, control voltage can do the same thing automatically.
