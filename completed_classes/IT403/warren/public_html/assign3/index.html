What is a Modular Synth?

A modular synth is a type of synthesizer that is designed as to use different components possibly from different manufaturers
in order to achieve a wieder variety of possibilities. There are many different types of modular synth, the focus of this blog will be
on the most popular form, Eurorack, which is a standardization of size and interoperability in this particular subset of modular synths

How do modular synths work?

The functionality of modular synts take inspiration from the earlies synths back in the 1970's and function using patch cables.
Today the idea of a synthesizer in most peoples head includes a keyboard with a series of knobs and buttons which change the sound of the keyboard.
In the case of keyboard synths, all of the connections between the various componenets that make up the synthesizer are hard wired beneaththe face of the synth.
In the case of modular synths, all of these connections are made manually by the user, which allows for more creativity and freedom in making music.

Wow, thats a lot of cables, whats going through them?

There are two types of signals which travel through the patch cable in a synthesizer, both in the form of voltage. The first is audio, which is an electical signal that is
oscillating at an audible rate. The second is control voltage, whitch can take the form of pulses, oscillations, or envelopes. The only difference between these
two types of signal is the frequency and its purpose. an audio signal is what is actually sent to the speakers for the user/audience to hear and control voltage is used to modulate
other signals, such as a filter (A filter which filters out high or low frequencies of audio) which can be modulated with contol voltage to go from off to fully on at a rate determined by the contol voltage.
A good way to think of contol voltage is to think of it as invisible fingers, in the same way one would slowly turn a knob to have some effect on the sound, control voltage can do the same thing
automatically
